Strait bills cloud customers by orchestration run. An orchestration run is customer work dispatched by Strait, including job runs and workflow step runs. Automatic system retries do not add runs. Manual replays do.

## Overage

Free is hard-capped at 5,000 included orchestration runs unless a credit card is added. Adding a card enables overage at $0.50 per 1,000 runs and applies the default $50 spending cap.

Starter, Pro, Scale, and Business include overage by default because a payment method is already attached to the subscription. Customers can disable overage from billing settings. With overage disabled, runs suspend at the included allowance and schedules pause until the next billing period or until overage is re-enabled.

At 80 percent of the spending cap, Strait emits `billing.cap_warning`. At 100 percent, Strait emits `billing.cap_reached`, suspends new dispatch, and pauses schedules. In-flight runs can finish. Suspension lifts when the cap is raised above current overage spend, the cap action is changed to notify-only, or the next billing period starts.

## Plan changes

Upgrades apply immediately. Downgrades apply at the next billing period. New workflow deployments must comply with the new plan's step cap, and new schedules must comply with the new schedule frequency cap.

## Self-hosted

The community edition is open source and has no cloud billing limits. Self-hosting requires PostgreSQL, Redis, and Sequin. The cloud dashboard, vendor support, hosted cloud orchestration, and BYO-cloud contracts are not included in community deployments.

See [Self-Hosting](/guides/self-hosting) for setup.
